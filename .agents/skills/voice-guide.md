# Voice Guide

Dylan's writing is conversational, practical, and direct. This guide is the authority on his voice.

## Do

- First person. Address the reader as "you."
- Start with the problem or the situation, not a thesis statement.
- Short paragraphs. Single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis are fine.
- Self-deprecating when natural.
- Real commands, real output, real errors. Show things step by step.
- Link generously: commits, PRs, issues, docs, external sources. Walls of text without inline references are boring.
- Parenthetical asides for color.
- End simply. No grand conclusions. Just stop when you're done.

## Don't

- Open with "In this article, we'll explore..." or "Let's dive in!" or any variation.
- Use these words: leverage, harness, unlock, empower, streamline, elevate, delve, foster, robust, seamless, cutting-edge, game-changer.
- Write an "In conclusion" section.
- Pad with filler.
- Passive corporate voice. "It was determined that..." — no. "I figured out that..." — yes.
- Exclamation marks for artificial enthusiasm. Only for genuine frustration, surprise, or humor.
- Bullet-point listicles without narrative flow.
- Fabricate Dylan's experiences, feelings, or opinions. If he didn't say it, don't write it.
- Long prose sections without inline evidence (links, output, screenshots, examples).

## AI Slop Patterns

These read as obviously AI-generated. Do not use them:

- `"Real data. Real insights. The tool delivers."` — staccato fragment hooks. Write a normal sentence.
- `"X is one thing, but Y is another."` — mirror formulas.
- `"The irony isn't lost on me"` / `"The hidden tax of"` / `"Let that sink in"` — overused AI phrases.
- `"Compare these two outputs:"` — instructional voice. Say "Here's what I mean:" or just show it.
- `"X tells you how A works. Y tells you how B works."` — parallel teacher-voice. Vary the structure.
- `"No external services. No setup. Just results."` — statement-statement-hook. Combine into a real sentence.
- `"Every. Single. Night."` — dramatic single-word sentences. Just say "every single night."
- `"The design lesson:"` / `"The key insight:"` — labels as transitions. Weave it in, don't label it.

## Em Dashes

Write them `like---this` (three hyphens, no spaces). Use sparingly. If you're reaching for one, try a comma, colon, parentheses, or restructuring first. More than ~3 per section means some should be something else.

## Structure Patterns

- **Tool/tech posts**: Problem → discovery → usage with real examples → tips/gotchas → brief wrapup
- **Opinion/reflection posts**: Anecdote → the insight → why it matters → brief tie-back
- **How-to posts**: What we're building → step by step with real output → troubleshooting → done

## Summaries

One or two plain sentences. What is this about and why should someone care?

Good:
- "Using `task` as a Make alternative for your automation tasks and wonderment."
- "How two German automotive engineers got inside my head."

Bad:
- "A comprehensive guide to leveraging Task for streamlined automation workflows."
- "Exploring the transformative power of mentorship in technology careers."
